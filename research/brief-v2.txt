01  PURPOSE


What this brief is for

This document is the handoff from the brand and exploratory UI phase (ChatGPT-rendered mocks + brand essence sheet) to the product UI design phase (Claude Design). It consolidates everything Claude Design needs to produce the Student Portal as a coherent product surface — not a collection of screens.

The ChatGPT exploration established the brand palette, portal-specific color system, typography pairing, and overall feel. It also produced ~40 student-facing screens covering most of the L1 navigation. This brief carries forward what worked, corrects what was wrong or premature, and fills the gaps.

This is a Student Portal-only brief. The other six portals (Instructor, Parent/Family, Admin, Community/Board of Education, Student Services, Public) are out of scope here and will receive their own handoffs.


02  PRODUCT


What LINKS is

LINKS is the product expression of the CES (Comprehensive Educational System) framework — a competency-based educational model authored by Dr. Marie Alcock-Hubley. CES treats learning as the demonstration of mastery against learning targets, not the accumulation of seat-time. LINKS is the K-12 platform that operationalizes that model across seven user-facing portals (Student, Instructor, Parent/Family, Admin, Community/Board of Education, Student Services, Public) plus a County/State/Federal reporting surface.

LINKS is engineered as a single, cross-portal data and intelligence layer with seven role-specific UIs on top. The cross-portal aspect is the strategic moat: the same data that shows a student their progress also gives an instructor the early-warning signal, an administrator the cohort pattern, and a parent the contextualized check-in — without compromising student privacy or introducing surveillance.

Beta target is September 2026, code-complete July 31, 2026, against the Gulliver Prep pilot cohort first. The Student Portal is the highest-stakes of the seven — it's the surface where the actual learning experience happens.


What the Student Portal is

The Student Portal is the student's daily home for school. Schedule, classes, work, progress, materials, tools, people, portfolio, and conversation — all in one surface, anchored by an AI Coach that knows the student's context. The portal is not a content delivery vehicle and not a grade book. It is the workspace and the record of growth.

It is used during class and outside it. On desktop and on mobile. By 9-12th graders in initial pilot. It must hold up to daily use by a teenager who has alternatives.


03  USERS


Who uses the Student Portal

This is the framing the ChatGPT pass got mostly right but worth restating explicitly.

PRIMARY  ·  K-12 Students

9th–12th grade in initial pilot, with capacity to extend down to elementary in subsequent versions. They span the full range of ability, learning differences, languages, and life circumstances. Some are competitive athletes, some are caregiving for siblings, some are managing IEPs or 504 plans, some are first-generation college-bound, some are just trying to get through the week. The portal must respect all of them as the primary operator of their own learning.

SECONDARY  ·  People in the student's life

Teachers, advisors, coaches, counselors, support staff, and family members appear contextually inside the Student Portal — but they don't operate it. They show up as named humans the student can message, schedule with, see updates from, and ask for help. Their own portals (Instructor, Parent, etc.) are where they do their work.

OUT OF FRAME  ·  Administrators and observers

County, district, and federal reporting bodies will eventually consume aggregated, privacy-preserving data from this portal. They never appear in the UI. The student should never feel watched.

CRITICAL FRAMING

The student is the center.  Their data, their work, their growth, their choices about who sees what. The Student Portal is built around their dignity, their autonomy as learners, and their voice. Surveillance language, infantilizing copy, and patronizing animation patterns are out of bounds.

05  CORRECTIONS


What the ChatGPT pass got wrong

Three corrections to the exploratory mocks. These are not stylistic preferences — they are product calls that change what Claude Design should render.

CORRECTION 01  ·  No Materio, no MUI

The previous LINKS design system was Materio (a Material UI / MUI template). It is deprecated. Engineering — Ricardo and Scott — uncovered substantial bloat and structural friction that became unworkable as the platform scaled. The ChatGPT mocks may carry residual Materio-shaped patterns (specific card chrome, MUI-style toolbar structures, etc.). Ignore those references. Claude Design should build from Tailwind primitives and the brand system above. No component library dependency.

CORRECTION 02  ·  Engagement framing is provisional

The ChatGPT mocks heavily feature gamification chrome: XP counters (&quot;1,250 / 2,000 XP&quot;), levels (&quot;Level 8 Explorer&quot;), streaks (&quot;12 days in a row!&quot;), and achievement badges. CES pedagogy is competency-based and mastery-oriented — not score-based. Whether this gamification language aligns with Dr. Alcock-Hubley's framework is an open pedagogical question that has not yet been resolved.

Render gamification chrome where it appears in the source mocks, but treat all of it as PROVISIONAL. Component-name it neutrally (e.g. ProgressIndicator rather than XPBar) so it can be revised without structural rework. Flag it explicitly in your component notes.

CORRECTION 03  ·  AI must never complete student work

Several mock surfaces — &quot;Check My Work,&quot; &quot;Writing Assistant,&quot; &quot;AI Tutor,&quot; &quot;Draft with AI&quot; — imply the AI may produce student work that the student then submits. This is not what LINKS AI does, and rendering it that way creates a product the school cannot adopt under academic integrity policy.

The LINKS AI Coach is a scaffold, never a substitute. Per CES principles and the platform's compliance posture:



It asks Socratic questions to help the student discover their own understanding.



It cites specific lessons, modules, and learning targets the student has already engaged with.



It surfaces comprehension gaps and offers targeted practice.



It generates derivative practice from the student's own work — quizzes, flashcards, concept maps — rather than producing the work itself.



It logs comprehension events and progress against learning targets.

It does not write essays. It does not solve problems. It does not produce work that submits as the student's own output. AI surfaces in the Student Portal must be designed against this constraint.

DESIGN IMPLICATION

Where the source mocks show the AI producing finished work — a complete essay draft, a solved math problem, a written message — re-render those surfaces with the AI in a coaching role instead. The student does the work; the AI helps them think about it.

Open IA questions

Two MCG-canonical concepts do not have an obvious home in the L1 list above. Claude Design should render against the recommended placements below; if a different structure feels stronger during render, surface the recommendation in component notes.

My Resources &amp; Support

The MCG L1 &quot;My Resources and Support&quot; covers: medical alerts, IEPs / IHP / 504, GATE, speech / physical / occupational therapy, day classes, resource specialist, one-on-one support, BOCES, ED/BD services, counseling, nurse, mental health, trauma support, family support, home health education, social workers, field study partnerships, and the School Attendance Review Board.

Recommendation:  Split. People-based services (counselor, nurse, social worker, resource specialist, etc.) live under My Team → Support Staff. Plan- and document-based items (IEP, 504, GATE eligibility, medical alerts) live under My Profile → Health &amp; Plans. The Student Portal is not the system of record for IEP authoring — it surfaces what's relevant to the student in plain language and provides routes to ask for help.

Assessment Data

The MCG L1 &quot;Assessment Data&quot; covers: SBAC / Smarter Balanced / PARC, district assessment data, portfolio data, 9-point goal attainment scale, progress reports, report cards, and official transcripts.

Recommendation:  Surface under My Progress as a sub-view (&quot;Assessment &amp; Records&quot;). The 9-point goal attainment scale is the canonical mastery view in CES — it should be visible at the top of My Progress, not buried.

09  WORKING PRINCIPLES


How to think about this product as you render

Competency over completion

CES is a competency-based framework. Mastery is the unit of progress, not seat-time and not letter grades. The Student Portal should foreground demonstrated growth (skills, learning targets, the 9-point goal attainment scale) over completion percentages and grade averages. Where the source mocks show &quot;GPA 3.72&quot; or &quot;75% complete,&quot; treat those as legacy framings that may need to evolve. Render them, but compose surfaces so the competency view is at least equally prominent.

AI as scaffold, never substitute

Restated from Correction 03: the AI in the Student Portal supports learning by asking questions, citing materials the student has already engaged with, and surfacing comprehension gaps. It does not produce work. AI surfaces should make the student's role visible and primary; the AI is a side panel, a drawer, a coach — not the protagonist.

Cross-portal intelligence is the moat

LINKS is not a single-portal product. The same data that informs a student's progress view feeds the instructor's early-warning, the parent's contextualized check-in, and the administrator's cohort pattern — without compromising student privacy. The Student Portal is the source of truth for the student's lived experience. Other portals subscribe to it.

Practical implication for design: surfaces in the Student Portal that show student-generated data (work, reflections, goals, comprehension events) should be designed knowing this data may inform other portals. The student should always see what's visible to whom.

FERPA + COPPA compliance is non-optional

LINKS is a K-12 product. Most of its users are minors. Student data is protected by federal law (FERPA, COPPA) and emerging state regulation. Compliance is not a UX layer added at the end — it is structural. Practical surface implications:



Sharing affordances must show what is shared, with whom, and why.



Privacy controls must be readable by a 14-year-old, not a lawyer.



Parental visibility is dual-purpose: required by law for younger students, harmful if absolute for older students. Surfaces involving family must surface what's visible to whom.

Mock-backed frontend is acceptable

During the build, frontend surfaces may be wired to mocked data while backend endpoints are still in flight. This is intentional. Claude Design should not over-rotate on backend completeness; it should produce production-quality UI against well-defined data shapes that engineering can wire later.


10  TECH STACK


What the rendered output should be built on

The deliverable should follow the same stack as the existing Curriculum Builder repo (ceslinks-design):



Next.js 16 (App Router) — for pages and routing.



React 19 — for components.



TypeScript — for type safety. No untyped JS files.



Tailwind CSS — for styling. Core utility classes only; no @apply abuse, no inline style props.



Lucide React — for icons. No alternative icon library.

No MUI. No Materio. No third-party component libraries beyond what is already in the Curriculum Builder repo. If a complex component is needed (a date picker, a rich text editor), build it from primitives or surface the dependency request in component notes — don't pull in a library independently.

12  WHAT NOT TO PRODUCE


Out of scope, intentionally



Other portals (Instructor, Parent/Family, Admin, Community/Board, Student Services, Public). Each will receive its own brief.



Mobile-first compositions. The portal is desktop-primary for beta, with responsive behavior expected. Mobile-only screens are out of scope.



Marketing pages, pricing tables, public-facing site chrome. Those belong to the Public Portal.



Alternate brand explorations. The brand system in Section 04 is the working canon. Surface deviations only with rationale, do not propose replacements.



AI surfaces depicting the AI completing student work. See Correction 03.



Surveillance UI — engagement-tracking dashboards visible to the student, attention scores, behavioral nudges. The student is the operator, not the subject.



Materio components, MUI components, or any third-party UI library beyond what's already in ceslinks-design.
